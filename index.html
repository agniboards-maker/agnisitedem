Acupressure FAQS

What is acupressure?

A practice rooted in Indian and Chinese medicine, thousands of years old. It works by applying pressure to specific points on the body - stimulating the nervous system, releasing tension, and shifting you into a state of calm. No needles. No recovery time. Just pressure.

What does acupressure actually do?

Your nervous system holds more than you think. Stress, tension, poor sleep - a lot of it lives in the body, not just the mind. Acupressure gives it somewhere to go. Regular use supports deeper sleep, reduced stress, and a body that feels less like it's carrying everything.

How do I use an Acupressure Board?

Place it on a flat surface. Step on with both feet - hold a wall if you're new to it. The intensity is immediate. Breathe through it. Within 30 seconds it shifts as your nervous system adjusts and begins to release. Two minutes is enough. Step off and feel the difference.

Can acupressure help with back pain?

Yes. The pressure points on the sole of the foot connect throughout the body. Standing on the board boosts circulation and triggers a release of built-up tension - which often shows up as back stiffness. It won't fix an injury, but if your back carries the weight of your day, you'll notice the difference.
